Introduction to Forex

Forex trading involves buying one currency while selling another simultaneously. This happens in currency pairs traded on the global forex market 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

Did You Know?

The forex market is the largest financial market in the world, with over $7.5 trillion traded daily. That's more than all stock markets combined!

Market Participants

The forex market consists of various participants, each with different motivations:

Participant Purpose Market Share
Central Banks Monetary policy implementation, currency intervention 5-10%
Commercial Banks Client services, proprietary trading, market making 40-50%
Corporations International trade, hedging currency risk 10-15%
Hedge Funds Speculation, arbitrage opportunities 10-15%
Retail Traders Speculation, investment 3-5%

How Trades Are Executed

Modern forex trading happens electronically through various platforms:

  • Interbank Market: Large banks trade directly with each other
  • Electronic Communication Networks (ECNs): Automated matching of buy/sell orders
  • Retail Brokers: Provide access to individual traders through online platforms
  • Market Makers: Provide liquidity by always being ready to buy or sell

Trade Execution Process

  1. Trader places order through broker platform
  2. Broker routes order to liquidity providers
  3. Order is matched with counterparty
  4. Trade is executed and confirmed
  5. Settlement occurs (usually T+2 days)

Key Forex Concepts

Term Definition Example
Pip Smallest price movement, usually 0.0001 EUR/USD moves from 1.1000 to 1.1001 = 1 pip
Spread Difference between bid and ask price EUR/USD Bid: 1.1000, Ask: 1.1002 = 2 pip spread
Leverage Borrowing funds to increase position size 1:100 leverage means $1,000 can control $100,000
Margin Required deposit to open leveraged position 2% margin = $2,000 needed for $100,000 position
