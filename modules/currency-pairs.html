What are Currency Pairs?

In forex trading, currencies are quoted in pairs. The first currency is the base currency, and the second is the quote currency. The value shows how much of the quote currency is needed to buy one unit of the base currency.

EUR/USD = 1.0850

EUR (Base Currency) / USD (Quote Currency)

This means 1 Euro = 1.0850 US Dollars

Reading Currency Pairs

Component Description Example
Base Currency The first currency in the pair (what you're buying/selling) In GBP/USD, GBP is the base
Quote Currency The second currency (what you're paying with) In GBP/USD, USD is the quote
Exchange Rate How much quote currency equals one unit of base currency GBP/USD = 1.2500 means 1 GBP = 1.25 USD

Types of Currency Pairs

Major Pairs

Most traded pairs involving USD and major economies. High liquidity, tight spreads.

  • EUR/USD - Euro vs US Dollar
  • GBP/USD - British Pound vs US Dollar
  • USD/JPY - US Dollar vs Japanese Yen
  • USD/CHF - US Dollar vs Swiss Franc
  • AUD/USD - Australian Dollar vs US Dollar
  • USD/CAD - US Dollar vs Canadian Dollar
  • NZD/USD - New Zealand Dollar vs US Dollar

Minor Pairs (Cross Pairs)

Major currencies paired together without USD. Moderate liquidity, wider spreads.

  • EUR/GBP - Euro vs British Pound
  • EUR/JPY - Euro vs Japanese Yen
  • GBP/JPY - British Pound vs Japanese Yen
  • EUR/CHF - Euro vs Swiss Franc
  • GBP/CHF - British Pound vs Swiss Franc
  • AUD/JPY - Australian Dollar vs Japanese Yen

Exotic Pairs

Major currency paired with emerging market currency. Lower liquidity, wider spreads.

  • USD/INR - US Dollar vs Indian Rupee
  • USD/TRY - US Dollar vs Turkish Lira
  • EUR/TRY - Euro vs Turkish Lira
  • GBP/INR - British Pound vs Indian Rupee
  • USD/ZAR - US Dollar vs South African Rand
  • USD/MXN - US Dollar vs Mexican Peso

Trading Characteristics

Pair Type Liquidity Spread Volatility Best For
Major Pairs Very High Very Tight (1-3 pips) Moderate Beginners, scalping
Minor Pairs High Moderate (3-6 pips) Moderate to High Experienced traders
Exotic Pairs Low Wide (10-50 pips) High Advanced traders, hedging
