Why Do Currency Exchange Rates Change?

Understanding Forex & Exchange Rates

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is a global marketplace for exchanging national currencies. Rates change due to multiple economic, geopolitical, and market factors influencing the supply and demand of a currency.

Key Factors Affecting Rates

Factor Description Impact on Currency
Interest Rates Central bank interest rates influence investor demand for a currency. Higher rates attract investors, increasing demand and value.
Inflation General rise in prices reduces purchasing power. Lower inflation tends to strengthen currency value.
Economic Indicators GDP growth, employment rates, trade balances. Strong indicators improve confidence and demand.
Global Events Political instability, wars, pandemics. Increases volatility and risk, affecting currency strength.
Market Sentiment Speculators’ perception of a currency's future. Positive sentiment increases demand; negative sentiment decreases it.

How Does Forex Work?

The Forex market operates 24/5 globally with currency pairs traded through brokers, banks, and financial institutions. It’s the largest liquid market, with daily volumes exceeding $6 trillion.

Key Forex Concepts Explanation
Bid & Ask Price Bid is the price a buyer is willing to pay; Ask is the price a seller wants.
Spread Difference between bid and ask price, representing broker profit.
Leverage Borrowing funds to increase trading position; high leverage increases risk and returns.
Pip Smallest price movement in a currency pair, usually 0.0001.
