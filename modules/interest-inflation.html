Understanding Interest Rates

Central banks set interest rates to control economic growth and inflation. Higher interest rates attract foreign capital, increasing demand for a currency, while lower rates tend to reduce its value.

How Interest Rates Affect Currency

  • Higher Rates: Attract foreign investment
  • Capital Inflow: Increases currency demand
  • Currency Appreciation: Value rises relative to others
  • Carry Trade: Investors borrow low-rate currencies to invest in high-rate ones

Central Bank Tools

  • Policy Rate: Benchmark interest rate
  • Forward Guidance: Communication about future policy
  • Quantitative Easing: Bond buying programs
  • Reserve Requirements: Bank lending controls

Understanding Inflation

Inflation reflects the rise in prices over time. Currencies with lower inflation rates tend to appreciate as purchasing power remains stable, while higher inflation weakens currency value.

Types of Inflation

  • Core Inflation: Excludes food and energy prices
  • Headline Inflation: Includes all price changes
  • Producer Inflation: Wholesale price changes
  • Wage Inflation: Rising labor costs

Inflation's Currency Impact

  • Low Inflation: Preserves purchasing power
  • Moderate Inflation: Sign of healthy economy
  • High Inflation: Erodes currency value
  • Deflation: Can signal economic weakness

Interest Rate Differentials

The difference between interest rates of two countries is a key driver of currency exchange rates. Investors seek higher yields, creating demand for higher-yielding currencies.

Trading Interest Rate Events

Before Rate Decisions

Markets price in expectations. Check central bank communications and economic data for clues about future policy.

During Announcements

High volatility expected. Rate changes, forward guidance, and press conferences all impact currency values.

After Decisions

Markets may reverse initial reactions as traders analyze the full implications of policy changes.

Long-term Trends

Rate cycles (tightening vs easing) create sustained currency trends lasting months or years.
