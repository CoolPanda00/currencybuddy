Understanding Interest Rates

Central banks set interest rates to control economic growth. Higher interest rates attract foreign capital, increasing demand for a currency, while lower rates tend to reduce its value.

Country Current Interest Rate (%) Impact
USA 5.25 Strengthens USD due to higher returns for investors.
India 6.5 Attracts capital inflow, supporting INR value.

Understanding Inflation

Inflation reflects the rise in prices over time. Currencies with lower inflation rates tend to appreciate as purchasing power remains stable, while higher inflation weakens currency value.

Country Inflation Rate (%) Impact
Japan 1.2 Stable, supporting Yen strength.
Turkey 45.0 High inflation devalues the Lira.

Interest Rates & Inflation

Interest rates and inflation have significant impacts on exchange rates. When a country's interest rates rise, it often attracts foreign capital, increasing demand for its currency and appreciating its value. Conversely, high inflation generally devalues a currency due to reduced purchasing power.

Factor Effect on Currency
High Interest Rates Attracts investors, strengthens currency
High Inflation Reduces currency value, deters investment
